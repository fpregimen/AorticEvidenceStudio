# Content Review Workflow

## Review sequence

1. Confirm the source identity.
2. Confirm publication or guideline version.
3. Open the original source.
4. Identify sections relevant to the evaluation question.
5. Extract only claims supported by the source.
6. Record the exact location.
7. Record numerical outcomes separately.
8. Record limitations.
9. Identify whether evidence is direct or indirect.
10. Verify every claim against the original source.
11. Perform specialist validation.
12. Mark the record ready for generated answers.

## Claim rules

- Use one narrowly worded claim per record.
- The citation must support the exact claim.
- Do not copy conclusions from a secondary review when the primary study is available.
- Do not turn an imaging endpoint into a mortality conclusion.
- Do not treat non-significant results as equivalence.
- Do not mix acute, subacute, and chronic populations.
- Do not mix uncomplicated and complicated TBAD.
- Do not mix randomized and observational evidence.
- Do not infer guideline recommendations from narrative discussion.
- Record uncertainty explicitly.

## Numerical data rules

Record population size, follow-up period, primary endpoint, mortality, aorta-related mortality, aortic remodeling, aortic diameter change, false-lumen thrombosis, reintervention, conversion, neurologic complications, rupture, and loss to follow-up separately. Do not add values unless they have been checked in the original source.

## Review-status definitions

- **Not started:** The review template exists but source content has not been examined.
- **In review:** Manual examination and structured extraction are underway.
- **Extraction completed:** Relevant claims and outcomes have been captured, but citation verification may remain.
- **Citation verified:** Every retained claim has exact supporting text and a traceable source location checked against the original.
- **Specialist validated:** A qualified specialist has reviewed interpretation and suitability for generated answers.
- **Requires correction:** A reviewer found a metadata, extraction, citation, or interpretation problem that must be resolved.

## Specialist validation

Manual specialist review is required for guideline recommendation wording, trial primary-endpoint interpretation, long-term outcome interpretation, acute-versus-subacute classification, high-risk feature selection, and any claim that influences treatment selection. No review record is ready for generated answers merely because extraction is complete.
